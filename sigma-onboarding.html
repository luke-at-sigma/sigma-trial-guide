Build Your First Workbook in Sigma

Lesson overview — Watch first (3 min) Introduction to Sigma workbooks and the canvas layout

Introduction

A workbook is your primary workspace in Sigma. It combines a spreadsheet-like interface with a drag-and-drop canvas, letting you explore data, build visualizations, and share insights — all without writing SQL.

In this lesson, you'll connect a sample dataset, build a table, add a chart, and publish your first workbook. By the end, you'll have a working dashboard ready to share with your team.

Before you start: Make sure you have access to at least one data connection. If you haven't set one up yet, visit Connections in the left rail and follow the setup guide.

Step 1 of 4

Open a new workbook

Start by creating a blank workbook from the left rail. Click Create new at the top of the navigation, then select Workbook from the menu.

Sigma opens the workbook editor with a blank canvas. The toolbar at the top gives you access to formatting, formula entry, and undo/redo. The canvas below is where you'll place charts, tables, and controls.

Step 2 of 4

Add a data source

Every element in Sigma is powered by a data source — a table, view, or custom SQL from your connected warehouse.

To add one:

  • In the canvas, click the Data button in the floating toolbar on the left edge.
  • Select Table from the element picker.
  • In the source panel on the right, browse your connection and select the SALES_ORDERS table (or any table from your warehouse).

Sigma loads a live preview of the table directly in your workbook — no data is moved or copied.

Step 3 of 4

Group and summarize your data

Sigma lets you transform your data directly in the canvas — no SQL required. You can group rows, add calculated columns, and apply aggregations in a few clicks.

Try it now:

  • Click the Region column header and select Group column from the menu.
  • Click Add column → Sum → select Order_Revenue.
  • Rename the new column to Total Revenue by double-clicking its header.

Your table now shows total revenue grouped by region — a live aggregate computed against your warehouse every time the workbook loads.

Group and summarize your table
Pro tip: Hold Shift and click multiple column headers to group by more than one dimension at once.

Step 4 of 4

Create a bar chart from your table

Once your data is grouped, you can turn it into a chart in one click. Sigma automatically infers the best axis mapping based on your column types.

To build a chart:

  • Click the Chart button in the toolbar above your table.
  • Select Bar chart from the chart picker.
  • Sigma maps Region to the X-axis and Total Revenue to the Y-axis automatically.
  • Use the Format tab in the right panel to apply a currency format ($) to the Y-axis labels.

Next steps

You've built your first workbook — a live, interactive dashboard connected directly to your data warehouse. No data was downloaded, no pipeline was built. Every change you make queries your warehouse in real time.

In the next lesson, you'll learn how to add filters and controls so viewers can slice the data themselves — turning a static dashboard into a self-service analytics tool.
