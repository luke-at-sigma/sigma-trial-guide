Navigate a Workbook as a View User

Understand the three workbook modes and what you can do in each — before making any edits.

Overview: Workbook modes in Sigma (4 min) View · Explore · Edit — what each mode unlocks

Before you edit: understand workbook modes

Every Sigma workbook has three modes: View, Explore, and Edit. When you first open a shared workbook, you land in View mode — the published state that everyone sees.

What you can do depends on the permission level you've been granted. But even with basic Can view access, you can interact with the data in meaningful ways without touching the published version.

Good to know: Any changes you make in View or Explore mode are isolated to your session. You can't accidentally break the shared workbook.

Step 1 of 5

Open and navigate a shared workbook

Open the Plugs Electronics Sample workbook from your Documents folder. Use the page tabs at the bottom to move between pages — each page contains a different view of the same underlying data.

The workbook toolbar at the top gives you access to:

  • Document menu — saved views, share, comments, schedule exports
  • Refresh — re-query the warehouse for the latest data
  • Help — documentation, community posts, QuickStarts

Step 2 of 5

Interact with controls and filters

Controls are interactive inputs — dropdowns, date pickers, sliders — that the workbook creator has placed on the canvas. They target one or more data elements and update results without entering Edit mode.

Try changing the Date Range control from "Last 12 months" to "Last 30 days." Watch the charts and tables update instantly — Sigma re-queries your warehouse in real time.

You can also right-click any cell in a table to use a quick filter to keep or exclude that specific value on the fly.

Filter types available: List, Top N, Number range, Date range, Text match, and Hierarchy — depending on what the workbook designer has exposed for interaction.

Step 3 of 5

Drill down into data

Many charts support click-through actions — clicking a bar or data point triggers a drill or filter on another element. Try clicking a product category in the bar chart and watch the detail table filter to match.

You can also maximize any element to full-screen by hovering over it and clicking the maximize icon in the top-right corner of the element.

Try drill-down in Sigma

Step 4 of 5

Save a custom view

Once you've set controls or filters you want to return to, save them as a saved view. Your saved view captures your filter state and is visible only to you — it doesn't affect the published workbook or what other users see.

To save a view: open the Document menu → Custom view → Save this custom view → give it a name. You can also set it as your default landing state for that workbook.

Screenshot: Document menu → Custom view → Save

Step 5 of 5

Export data

Viewers with Can explore access can download table data to CSV or Excel, or export the full workbook to PDF. The option appears in the element's action menu (three dots) or via the Document menu.

If you don't see the export option, your admin may have restricted it at the account type level. Check with your Sigma administrator.
