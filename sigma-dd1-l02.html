Navigate a shared workbook

Find your way around pages, elements, and the workbook toolbar as a View user.

Navigating workbooks (4 min) Pages · Toolbar · Document menu
Step 1

Page tabs

Workbooks are organized into pages. Click the tabs at the bottom of the screen to move between pages. Each page is a different view — often organized by audience, such as Executive Summary, Regional Detail, or Operations.

If a workbook has many pages, you may see a scroll arrow at the ends of the tab bar. Click it to reveal additional pages that are out of view.

Step 2

The workbook toolbar

At the top of the workbook you'll see the workbook name, a mode indicator (View / Explore / Edit), a Refresh button to pull the latest data, and a Document menu (three-dot icon). As a View user, the Edit button will not appear — only View and, if permitted, Explore.

The mode indicator shows which mode you're currently in. Click View to return to the published state at any time.

Step 3

Document menu

Click the three-dot menu next to the workbook name to access key actions:

  • Saved views: switch to or manage your personal saved filter states
  • Export: download as CSV or PDF
  • Schedule: view schedules you're subscribed to (receive only)
  • Comments: read and add comments on the workbook
  • Sharing: see who has access to this workbook
Step 4

Scrolling and resizing

Elements on a page are fixed by the workbook creator — you cannot reposition them. You can scroll vertically if the page content is taller than your screen.

To get a closer look at any chart or table, hover over it and click the expand icon that appears in the top-right corner. This maximizes the element to full screen. Press Escape or click the X to return to the normal view.

Reload required? If you see a "Reload required" banner at the top of the workbook, it means the workbook has been updated by the owner since you opened it. Click Reload to see the latest published version. Your active filters will reset.
